A Plague Tale: Innocence

On Google as Razor Chuckles

Picked this up on a Steam sale for a great price so I'd have something different to play this weekend, and it was perfect for a change of pace. It's primarily a stealth game with a few puzzles, all set in a dark, depressing plague-ridden Medieval France. The story takes a while to develop, but it's solid and enjoyable. The characters are well-voiced (and you can choose English, French or German audio with English subtitles for more immersion). A Plague Tale in many ways reminded me of the old Thief games from the 90's, but with exceptional graphics and smarter AI. There's also a touch of Tomb Raider here as well.
It's refreshing to have a protagonist as a girl of about 15 who isn't sexualized or possessing ungodly combat prowess. The majority of the game she is trying to avoid detection as well as solving a few puzzles, though she is able to take out some enemies from a distance with her sling. The game is very linear, but that's fine with me because it's all about the environment and the story. I never found any situation so difficult that I couldn't progress after a couple of tries (until the very end, but it's a fun ending so I won't spoil it).
Overall I would give it a 7/10, which in my opinion is a very respectable score. The only negatives I found are that the movement can be a bit clunky and a couple of times I found myself not knowing exactly what my objective was or where I was supposed to go - but those are minor. I completed the game in about 12-15 hours, and it has 16 unique chapters plus a very short epilogue after the credits. As much as I enjoyed the dark environment and theme, and some very unique stealth situations and puzzles, I'm not sure the game has a lot of replayability. But that's fine, since games like this are meant to be experienced, not repeated over and over.

Little Nightmares

On Google as Radical Batz

I was blown away by yet another masterpiece made by Tarsier Studios. This game has more length, better graphics than it's first predecessor and they kept the original style that Little Nightmares always charmed for. While the first little nightmares takes place in one environment which is the maw, Little Nightmares 2 this takes place in various of areas. In my opinion the game was of good length, it took me 7-8 hours to beat because I explored as much as I could, and took my time with my playthrough and did die a couple of times or got stuck in some parts but overall it's great. The hat collectibles and finding all the Glitching kids was such a cool idea, it made exploring all the more worth it. On my 2nd playthrough already and got all the achievements which was so much fun, the secret achievements you need to get are so creative, I had to look up a guide to see how to get them. The art team did an amazing job on this, the entire team did but the art department outdone themselves, alot of work has been put into this game and I will look forward whenever Little Nightmares 3 makes an appearance! :)

Red Dead Redemption 2

On Google as Tyrannosaurus Flex

Masterful storytelling, oscar worthy voice acting, a soundtrack you could listen to over and over, an open world that feels alive but wants you dead, characters you wish you could confide in and cuddle, graphics that have truly set the bar for open world games and an experience you will never forget.
Red Dead 2 is a masterpiece on so many levels. Rockstar games manage to out do themselves with every new title they release, which in hindsight seems completely impossible, for them however it's just challenge accepted and wait and see. The Houser Brothers are militant geniuses, their childhoods have truly shaped them into the gaming pioneers they have so vehemently become. Their love for the cinematic experience and thirst for havoc wreaking fun have truly illuminated Rockstar games as the gaming juggernauts that they are and as far as I'm concerned is unrivalled by any other developer who dare to produce a truly inspiring open world experience. Let's just thank that great big gamer in the sky for Rockstar games and may they live forever.

The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild

On Google as Minidu Perera

The legend of zelda breath of the wild easily no game has ever come with such a superb open world with mind blowing gameplay and creative puzzles. The amount of detail for a game which runs on such a low end console is just amazing. Even though the story isn't the best, it has probably one of the best introductions in a video game of all time. The atmosphere of the game is something which only a very few games have achieved with the story about how the kingdom of hyrule met destruction with the appearance of Gannon, it manages to tell of how it was destroyed with the very detailed but also lifeless world. Botw is a game which can be considered a truly open world game with how much freedom you have in every single aspects except the shrines. The npcs are also very detailed and the game's difficulty is also well balanced. It was such a successful game that many game studios have taken inspiration from it and it has won the most number of game awards ever. It's a game which will never feel outdated because of how much it influenced the video game industry but also how much it will make an impact in the future.
